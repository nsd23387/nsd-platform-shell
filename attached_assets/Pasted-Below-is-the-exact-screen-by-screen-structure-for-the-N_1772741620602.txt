Below is the exact screen-by-screen structure for the NSD Marketing Command Center, aligned to Hormozi’s Core 4 and designed to support two perspectives (Operator and Executive) while still allowing deep drill-down into SEO and Google Ads.

No fluff. This is the blueprint.

Each screen uses the same interaction model:

Date range selector

Comparison toggle (previous period, WoW, MoM)

Channel filter (all, SEO, Google Ads, direct, referral, email, other)

Drilldown breadcrumb (Core 4 → Channel → Campaign → Asset)

Screen 1: Executive Overview (CEO View)
Purpose

“Are we growing profitably, and which engine is driving it?”

Layout

Top KPI Bar

Revenue (actual)

Pipeline value (quotes)

Marketing spend

CAC (blended)

ROAS / MER

Orders (or wins)

Quote close rate

Avg order value

Core 4 Performance Tiles (4 cards)
Each tile shows:

Spend (if applicable)

Leads/quotes

Revenue/pipeline

CAC / cost per quote

Trend arrow (7d, 30d)

Revenue by Engine (stacked trend)

Warm outreach

Cold outreach

Free content

Paid ads

What changed? (Insights feed)
Auto-generated alerts:

CPC up/down

Impression share loss

SEO top query moved from pos 11 → 8

Quote-to-close rate shifted

Budget capped early

Drilldown

Click any Core 4 tile → goes to that engine screen.

Screen 2: Operator Hub (Daily Control Room)
Purpose

“What should we do today to improve performance?”

Layout

Today KPIs

Spend today vs budget pacing

Quotes today

CPA / cost per quote today

Top converting keyword/query today

Lead quality proxy (avg quote value, business vs personal)

Action Queue (priority list)

Add negatives (from search terms)

Raise budget (if Lost IS budget > threshold)

Adjust bids (if Lost IS rank > threshold)

SEO: update titles/meta for high impressions low CTR

SEO: publish content for pos 8–20 opportunities

Watchlist

Campaigns capped by budget

Keywords with high spend, zero conversions

Landing pages with high traffic, low quote rate

Screen 10: Attribution & Data Health (Non-Negotiable)
Purpose

Prevent making decisions on broken data.

KPIs

% sessions with channel source

% conversions with landing page

% conversions with gclid/utm

Cross-domain join health (site → quote)

Spend ingestion freshness (Google Ads)

Event ingestion freshness (web events)

Visuals

Data completeness trend lines

“Red flag” alerts when attribution drops

Dynamic Requirements (What the Dashboard Must Support)
Universal Controls

Interaction Requirements

Drilldowns (Core 4 → engine → campaign → ad group → keyword → term)

Trend lines for every KPI (not single numbers)

Table sorting + column filters

Alerts/flags (budget capped, rank loss, CTR drops)

Visualization Requirements

Time-series charts

Stacked trends (engine contribution)

Funnels (click → quote → close)

Opportunity tables (queries/pages)

Pacing charts (spend by hour)

Where SEO and Google Ads Fit in Core 4

SEO is the primary engine inside Post Free Content

Google Ads is the primary engine inside Run Paid Ads

Both have their own operator screens, but roll up into Executive/Core 4 summaries.
